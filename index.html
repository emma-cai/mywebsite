About Me

I am a PhD student at Department of Computer & Information Science, Temple University. I started my graduate program in 2011. My advisor is Dr. Alexander Yates. I am interested in Machine Learning, Natural Language Processing and its applications. I am currently working on large-scale textual schema matching and applying the technique to large-scale semantic parsing and question answering.


Projects

FreeParser is a semantic parsing system, translating natural language (NL) sentences to formal meaning representation (MR) language.
NL: Who are Bill Gates parents?
MR: λx. (person_parents(bill_gates, x))
Taken Freebase as a knowledge database, FreeParser can be directly applied to a question answering system, providing a next-generation search and information retrieval capability. Following, the system would return a list contianing the set of matching components: Mary Maxwell Gates and William H. Gates, Sr.
